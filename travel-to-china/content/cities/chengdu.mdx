# Chengdu — Land of Abundance

## The Heavenly State

Chengdu (成都) has been known as the "Land of Abundance" (天府之国) for over 2,000 years. It's a city that takes life slowly — where drinking tea, playing mahjong, and eating well are daily rituals, not luxuries.

> **Motto:** "Once you come to Chengdu, you'll never want to leave" — and the locals genuinely believe it.

## Top Attractions

### Chengdu Research Base of Giant Panda Breeding (大熊猫繁育研究基地)
The **#1 reason** most travelers visit Chengdu. Home to over 200 giant pandas, including adorable cubs.

- **Best time:** 8:00 AM — pandas are most active during morning feeding
- **Time needed:** 3–5 hours
- **Highlights:** Cubs enclosure, red pandas, panda nursery
- **Getting there:** 40 min by taxi from city center (~¥50)

### Jinli Ancient Street (锦里)
A beautifully restored ancient street lined with traditional Sichuan-style buildings, craft shops, and food stalls. Magical in the evening with red lanterns lit.

### Wuhou Shrine (武侯祠)
Dedicated to Zhuge Liang, the legendary strategist of the Three Kingdoms period. The adjacent Jinli Street makes this a combined visit.

### Wide and Narrow Alleys (宽窄巷子)
Three restored Qing Dynasty-era alleyways — Kuan Alley (wide), Zhai Alley (narrow), and Jing Alley (well). Now filled with tea houses, restaurants, and boutique shops.

### Dujiangyan Irrigation System
A UNESCO World Heritage Site built in **256 BC** that still functions today. This ancient engineering marvel tamed the Min River and turned Sichuan into the fertile "Land of Abundance."

## Sichuan Cuisine in Chengdu

### The Spice Experience
Sichuan food isn't just about heat — it's about the **málà** (麻辣) sensation: the numbing tingle of Sichuan peppercorn combined with fiery chili.

### Hot Pot Culture
Hot pot is a social ritual in Chengdu. The city has over **10,000 hot pot restaurants**. The signature is the **nine-square grid pot** (九宫格) with different zones for cooking different ingredients.

> **Traveler's Tip:** The best hot pot restaurants (like Huangcheng Laoma) don't adjust spice levels. Want it mild? Order Yuanyang (鸳鸯锅) — half spicy, half clear broth.

## Teahouse Culture

Chengdu has more teahouses than any other city in the world. Visit a traditional teahouse for the complete experience:

- **Renmin Park (People's Park)** — Heming Teahouse, the most famous
- **Wangjianglou Park** — Bamboo groves and riverside tea
- **Wenshu Monastery Tea House** — Buddhist temple tea house

Don't be surprised to see **ear cleaning** (掏耳朵) services offered in teahouses — it's a uniquely Chengdu tradition!

## Suggested Itinerary (3 Days)

- **Day 1:** Panda Base (morning) → Wenshu Temple → Wide & Narrow Alleys → Hot pot dinner
- **Day 2:** Dujiangyan day trip → Jinli Ancient Street evening
- **Day 3:** People's Park & teahouse → Sichuan Cuisine Museum → Leshan Giant Buddha

> **Survival Tip:** Get a tissue packet before meals — many local restaurants don't provide napkins. And pace yourself with the spice; the "ring of fire" is real.
