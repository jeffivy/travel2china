# China's Size and Administrative Regions

## A Vast Territory

China is the **third-largest country in the world** by total area, spanning approximately **9.6 million square kilometers** (3.7 million square miles). It stretches about 5,200 km from east to west and 5,500 km from north to south.

> **Size comparison:** China is roughly the same size as the United States, and slightly larger than Brazil or Australia.

## Administrative Divisions

China has a multi-tiered administrative structure:

### Provincial Level (省级)
- **23 Provinces** — Including Guangdong, Sichuan, Yunnan, Fujian
- **5 Autonomous Regions** — Guangxi, Inner Mongolia, Ningxia, Tibet, Xinjiang
- **4 Municipalities** — Beijing, Shanghai, Tianjin, Chongqing
- **2 Special Administrative Regions** — Hong Kong, Macau

### Prefecture Level (地级)
- Prefecture-level cities
- Autonomous prefectures
- Prefectures

### County Level (县级)
- Counties
- County-level cities
- Districts
- Autonomous counties

## Geographical Features

China's geography is incredibly diverse:

- **Mountains** cover about 33% of China's land area (Himalayas, Kunlun, Tian Shan)
- **Plateaus** account for 26% (Tibetan Plateau — "Roof of the World")
- **Basins** cover 19% (Tarim Basin, Sichuan Basin)
- **Plains** cover 12% (North China Plain, Northeast Plain)
- **Hills** cover 10%

### Major Rivers
- **Yangtze River** (长江) — 6,300 km, the longest in Asia
- **Yellow River** (黄河) — 5,464 km, "Cradle of Chinese Civilization"
- **Pearl River** (珠江) — 2,400 km, flowing through Guangzhou
- **Mekong River** (澜沧江) — originates in Tibet, flows to Southeast Asia

## Travel Implications

Understanding China's size helps with trip planning:

1. **Internal flights** are often necessary for multi-city itineraries
2. **High-speed trains** connect major cities efficiently
3. **Climate varies dramatically** — check regional weather before packing
4. **Cultural differences** between regions can feel like visiting different countries
5. **Language dialects** vary widely, though Mandarin is universally understood
